# Paul/Elder Six Types of Socratic Questions

Source: Richard Paul's classical taxonomy (as adapted in *The Thinker's Guide to the Art of Socratic Questioning*).

Every grill question generated by this skill MUST be classifiable under exactly one of these types. If a question fits none, it is not Socratic — drop it.

The critic uses this axis together with the quality axes in `quality-axes.md` (see `critic-rubric.md`).

---

## 1. Clarification

**Probes:** what something means, restatement, the precise term.

**Stems:**
- 你说的 X 是什么意思？ / What do you mean by X?
- 能换一种说法吗？ / Could you put that another way?
- 你的核心点是 A 还是 B？ / Is your basic point A or B?
- 能给一个例子吗？ / Could you give me an example?
- 有没有更精确的术语？ / Can you find a more precise term for X?

**When useful:** the user's understanding has a vague term doing too much work. E.g. "guidance" without specifying classifier-free vs classifier-based.

---

## 2. Assumption

**Probes:** what is being taken for granted, what could be assumed instead.

**Stems:**
- 你在假设什么？ / What are you assuming?
- 可以反过来假设什么？ / What could we assume instead?
- 你似乎假设 X，对吗？ / You seem to be assuming X. Do I understand correctly?
- 怎么证成这个 X 是理所当然的？ / How would you justify taking X for granted?
- 为什么有人会做出这个假设？ / Why would someone make this assumption?

**When useful:** the user's mental model rests on an unstated invariant (e.g., "guidance scale should always be positive"). Probing the assumption reveals the load-bearing claim.

---

## 3. Reasons and Evidence

**Probes:** how the user knows, what supports the claim, what would change their mind.

**Stems:**
- 你怎么知道？ / How do you know?
- 有什么证据？ / Do you have any evidence?
- 什么会改变你的想法？ / What would change your mind?
- 怎么找出 X 是不是真的？ / How could we find out whether X is true?
- 你怎么回应认为 ¬X 的人？ / What would you say to someone who said ¬X?

**When useful:** the user has stated a belief but hasn't located the evidence. This is the gateway to the Probe & Evidence layer — every type-3 question should bind to a concrete probe.

---

## 4. Perspectives / Alternatives

**Probes:** other viewpoints, counter-arguments, comparisons, who benefits.

**Stems:**
- 还有什么替代方案？ / What's an alternative?
- 别的方法怎么看？ / What's another way to look at it?
- 反方观点是什么？ / What's a counter-argument?
- 这个方法的强项和弱项？ / What are the strengths and weaknesses?
- 与 Y 相比呢？ / Compare X with Y.

**When useful:** for mechanism revelation specifically, "why this form, not the obvious alternative?" is one of the most discriminative questions in the entire taxonomy.

---

## 5. Consequences / Implications

**Probes:** what follows if the claim is true; downstream effects.

**Stems:**
- 你这么说，是不是在暗示 Y？ / When you say X, are you implying Y?
- 如果这成立，还有什么也必须为真？ / If this is true, what else must also be true?
- 如果发生了，结果会是？ / If that happened, what else would happen as a result?
- 是必然，还是只是可能？ / Would that necessarily happen or only probably?
- 任何推论会让你反过来想吗？ / Would any implication cause you to think differently?

**When useful:** for testing mental model integrity. If the user's explanation is correct, X must follow; if X doesn't follow, the explanation has a leak.

---

## 6. Meta-question ("Questions about the question")

**Promoted to first-class type per MVP design — was a metadata field in PRD.**

**Probes:** why we're asking this, what the answer would change, what's at stake.

**Stems:**
- 为什么这个问题重要？ / Why does this question matter?
- 如果你回答了，下一步会怎么变？ / If you answered this, what would change next?
- 问错了问题会发生什么？ / What goes wrong if we're asking the wrong question?
- 这个问题假设了什么？ / What does the question itself assume?
- 这个问题和我们核心目标有什么关系？ / How does this question relate to our main goal?

**When useful:** when the user is about to commit to a long investigation. Asking "why are we asking this?" often saves the entire investigation by revealing it was the wrong question. Also a powerful self-check during candidate generation — Claude should ask this *about its own candidates* before sending them.

---

## Distribution rule

For a 5-question budget, aim for **at least 3 different Paul-types** represented in the kept set. A grill that's all clarification questions or all evidence questions is a tell that the critic didn't push hard enough on diversity.

A common high-impact pattern for mechanism revelation:
- Q1: clarification (pin down the term)
- Q2: assumption (surface the hidden premise)
- Q3: perspective (the "why not the alternative?" question)
- Q4: consequence (test integrity of the explanation)
- Q5: evidence (demand the verifying material)
